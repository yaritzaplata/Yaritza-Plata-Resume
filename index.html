I grew up in Delano, California, a city full of agriculture and hard working people. I had the chance to join the FFA in my high school where I raised livestock for three years and was in leadership teams in both chapter and sectional level. Because of the FFA, I have experience with vet practices, public speaking, and teamwork. I am currently doing an internship under National Her Campus as a Campus Expansion Assistant where I communicate with people from different schools all over the country and ask them if they're interested in opening a Her Campus chapter at their school. Her Campus has helped me feel like I belong at UCR so I hope that I help be part of the process that makes others feel that way. My weekly tasks involve communicating with staff and students, sending over 50 messages a week, and replying to those interested. This internship has taught me communication and persistence as not everyone is going to reply.

I now attend UC Riverside as a Creative Writing major where I have work to improve my writing. At UCR, I am able to practice fiction, non-fiction, and poetry to be a well-rounded writer. I have submitted to multiple publications and magazines. I submit works of fiction and occasionally poetry even when it's not my main focus. I also self-publish online. Writing is what's going to be my career, and it's a powerful tool in the world.

I have currently written a novel, and I'm working on my second one. I also write poetry and short stories and hope that in future, I can start my own literary magazine. This literary magazin would be for writers in minority groups. I want it to be a space for people to feel included in the publishing industry. When I graduate, I want to go into the publishing world and be either a writer or an editor. I want to help put books o the shelves for children and teenagers that are in underepresented minority groups also because it's important to know that you are not alone.
